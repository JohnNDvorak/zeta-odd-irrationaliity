Grassmannian-style window invariants built from packet windows; the strongest nonlinear direction tried so far.

Normalized Plücker objects track subspace geometry of packet windows in a nonlinear, projectively meaningful way. This
family produced the first clear frontier improvements beyond packet and transfer maps.

## Banked behavior

- Five-term normalized Plücker improved the old chart frontier.
- Six-term normalized Plücker improved it again.
- Quotient continuations are weaker than the full normalized object.
- On the six-window object, the cheap recurrence-level families now split cleanly:
  - same-index and first-difference families fail after fitting
  - the canonical free-zero support-1 family is inconsistent on the fit block

So the next move in this geometry is a genuinely different recurrence-level family, not another quotient or support
depth increase.
